# 09 — 2024–2026 Updates (Helpful Content, Core Updates, AI Overviews)

## SEO Starter Guide refresh (Feb 2024)

Google simplified its beginner guidance — useful signal about what actually matters:
- **Removed** the structured-data section ("advanced topic, beginners don't need it; CMS plugins handle it").
- **Reduced** image guidance to essentially **alt text**.
- **Removed** the dedicated 404 guidance ("do whatever makes sense for users").
- **Removed** the standalone navigation section → emphasized **aggressively internal-linking important URLs**.
- **Removed** the mobile-friendliness section (most platforms are already responsive).
- **Added** a "common SEO myths" section — anti-patterns to ignore.

## Core updates & Helpful Content

- ⚠️ Core updates are **broad ranking-system recalibrations run several times a year**, *not* penalties targeting your site. A drop is often **relative** (others improved), not a strike.
- **Aug 2022 Helpful Content Update** → "people-first" content; folded into quarterly quality signals through 2024–2025.
- **Mar 2024 core update** → aimed to cut low-quality/unhelpful content ~40%; new spam policies; 45-day rollout.
- **Aug 2024 core update** → rewarded quality, demoted low-value SEO content.
- **Site Reputation Abuse** policy (May 2024, updated Nov 2024) → targets "parasite SEO" (established domains hosting third-party content).
- **Recovery:** don't make rumor-driven "quick fixes" or delete content as a first move. Focus site-wide on **helpful, reliable, people-first** content; use Google's *creating-helpful-content* self-assessment. Wait at least a week after a rollout finishes before analyzing; recovery can take **months**.

## AI Overviews / AI Mode (formerly SGE)

- ✅ **No separate opt-in.** To appear, a page must simply be **indexed and eligible to show in Search with a snippet** — same eligibility as normal Search.
- ⚠️ **No special files or markup are needed or used:** Google ignores `llms.txt`, "AI text" files, and Markdown versions. Structured data is **not required** for AI features.
- ✅ Write for humans — well-organized, original, expertise-driven content beats commodity/AI-rehashed text. No need to chop content into tiny "AI chunks."
- Practical signals (third-party measurements, not Google guarantees): AI Overview sources overwhelmingly come from the **top 10 organic results** — so classic ranking remains the prerequisite. List/bulleted formatting is frequently cited. Being cited can **lift CTR**; an AI Overview without your citation can **lower** it.

## The durable takeaway

None of this replaces fundamentals. Ship indexable, server-rendered, well-structured, original content with correct canonical/hreflang/robots/sitemap/structured-data — then AI features and core updates take care of themselves.
